AIR QUALITY

Air quality refers to how clean or polluted the air is.

Good air quality means the air is safe to breathe, while poor air quality means it has harmful pollutants like smoke, dust, and chemicals that can affect health.










How Air Quality Affects Ecosystems



1.Harm to Plants:



1.1. Damaged Leaves: Polluted air can harm plants by

damaging their leaves and reducing their ability to grow.




2.Water Contamination:




2.1. Pollutant Deposition: Pollutants from the air can settle on

soil and water, harming animals and plants living in these environments.







How Air Quality Affects Climate Change







1.Greenhouse Gases:



Heat Trapping: Gases like carbon dioxide (CO₂) and methane trap

heat in the atmosphere, contributing to global warming and climate change.



2.Aerosols



Cooling or Warming: Some air pollutants, called aerosols,

can either cool or warm the Earth depending on their type, adding complexity to climate change effects.









How Air Quality Affects Wildfires







1.Increased Fire Risk:



Dry Conditions: Air pollution can lead to less rain and drier conditions,

increasing the likelihood of wildfires.



2.More Smoke:



Fire Emissions: When wildfires occur, they release smoke

and harmful particles, worsening air quality even more.



Interconnectedness of Air Quality, Ecosystems, Wildfires, and Climate Change







1.Air Quality and Ecosystems:



Polluted air can harm plants, animals,

and water bodies, disrupting entire ecosystems.

2.Air Quality and Wildfires:



Poor air quality makes it easier for dry conditions to persist,

leading to more wildfires, which in turn worsen air pollution.

3.Air Quality and Climate Change:



Air pollutants can increase global warming or cooling,

while climate change can create conditions that worsen air quality.
