WILDFIRE

A wildfire is an uncontrolled fire that spreads quickly across forests, grasslands, or other dry areas.

Wildfires can start from lightning, human activities, or other natural causes and can cause widespread damage.







How Wildfires Affect Air Quality



1.Smoke and Pollutants



Air Pollution: Wildfires release smoke

and harmful particles into the air, making it harder to breathe and affecting health.




2.Long-Range Effects




Smoke Spread: Smoke can travel hundreds of miles, affecting air quality in distant areas, even if the fire is far away.







How Wildfires Affect Ecosystems







1.Loss of Habitat



Destroyed Homes for Animals: Wildfires burn trees and plants,

destroying habitats and forcing animals to flee.



2.Plant Regrowth



Slow Recovery: After a wildfire, it can take years for plants and trees to grow back,

which impacts the entire ecosystem.







How Wildfires Affect Climate Change







1.Release of CO₂



Carbon Emissions: Wildfires release large amounts of carbon dioxide (CO₂)

into the atmosphere, contributing to climate change.



2.Loss of Carbon Absorbers



Fewer Trees: Trees absorb CO₂, but when they burn,

they can no longer help reduce greenhouse gases, making climate change worse.







Interconnectedness of Wildfires, Air Quality, Ecosystems, and Climate Change







1.Wildfires and Air Quality



Wildfires create smoke that pollutes the air, affecting people's health and the environment.



2.Wildfires and Ecosystems



Fires destroy habitats and biodiversity, making it hard for ecosystems to recover.



3.Wildfires and Climate Change



Wildfires add CO₂ to the atmosphere

and reduce the number of trees that can absorb it, worsening climate change
