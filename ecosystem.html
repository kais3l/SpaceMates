ECOSYSTEM

An ecosystem is a community of living things, like plants, animals, and microorganisms, interacting with each other and their environment (like air, water, and soil). Ecosystems can be as large as a forest or as small as a pond.







How Ecosystems Affect Air Quality



1.Plants Clean the Air:



Absorbing Pollutants: Plants and trees absorb carbon dioxide (CO₂)

and other pollutants, helping to keep the air clean.




2.Pollution Impact




Harmed Ecosystems: When ecosystems are damaged,

like during deforestation, there are fewer plants to clean the air, leading to poorer air quality.







How Ecosystems Affect Climate Change







1.Carbon Absorption:



CO₂ Storage: Forests, wetlands, and oceans absorb carbon dioxide from the air,

which helps reduce the impact of climate change.



2.Ecosystem Loss:



Fewer Natural Absorbers: When ecosystems are destroyed by things like deforestation

or wildfires, less CO₂ is absorbed, which speeds up climate change.



How Ecosystems Affect Wildfires







1.Fuel for Fires:



- Dry Vegetation: In dry ecosystems, like grasslands or forests,

plants can become fuel for wildfires, making them spread faster.



2.Ecosystem Recovery:



- Slow Regrowth: After a wildfire, it can take years for the ecosystem to regrow

and return to balance, affecting plants, animals, and their habitat.



How Ecosystems Affect Droughts







1.Water Cycle Regulation:



- Plants Keep Water in the Soil: Healthy ecosystems

help store water in the soil, which can prevent droughts from getting worse.



2.Loss of Vegetation:



- Worsened Droughts: When ecosystems are damaged by

deforestation or fires, there are fewer plants to retain water, making droughts worse.



Interconnectedness of Ecosystems, Air Quality,

Climate Change, Wildfires, and Drought







1.Ecosystems and Air Quality:



- Plants clean the air, but damaged ecosystems

lead to more pollution and worse air quality.



2.Ecosystems and Climate Change:



- Healthy ecosystems absorb CO₂ and help slow climate change,

but when they are harmed, they release more carbon into the atmosphere.
