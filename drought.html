DROUGHT

Drought is when an area gets much less rain than usual, causing water shortages for plants, animals, and people.

Droughts can last for months or years and affect the environment and resources.










How Drought Affects Wildfires



1.Dry Vegetation



Fuel for Fires: Without rain, plants dry up and can catch fire more easily.




2.Longer Fire Seasons




Extended Drought: Drought can make fire seasons longer, with dry conditions lasting for months.







How Drought Affects Air Quality







1.Wildfire Smoke



More Fires, More Smoke: Drought increases wildfires,

which fill the air with smoke, making it hard to breathe.



2.Dust in the Air



Dry Ground: Drought creates dry, dusty conditions,

and winds can carry dust, reducing air quality.







How Drought Affects Climate Change







1.Less Plant Growth



Fewer Plants Absorbing CO₂: Drought reduces plant growth, so less CO₂ is absorbed, worsening climate change.



2.Drought-Induced Wildfires



More CO₂ from Fires: Wildfires during drought release CO₂, contributing to climate change.







Interconnectedness of Drought, Wildfires, Air Quality, Ecosystems, and Climate Change







1.Drought Leading to Wildfires



Drought dries plants, creating fuel for wildfires, which release smoke and harm air quality.



2.Drought and Ecosystem Struggles



Water shortages hurt plants and animals, leading to biodiversity loss and more wildfire risks.



3.Climate Change and Drought



Climate change worsens droughts, and droughts,

in turn, increase wildfires and CO₂ levels, making climate change worse.
